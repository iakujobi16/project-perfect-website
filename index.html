About

Class projects are hard - papers are lost and help is sparse. Teachers and students alike find it hard to create and complete class projects. Our solution to this problem is a tablet app that streamlines class projects from teacher creation to student submission. Happy teachers, happy students!

Needfinding

We began our project by brainstorming problem domains related to the learning and education space, which was particularly interesting to our team. We then decided to focus on the problem domains for elementary schools, which often have a lack of support and resources though they serve as the foundation for all higher education. With three problem domains in mind - information flow, classroom management, and building friendly environments - we interviewed 6 random students, parents, and teachers and talked to them about what they felt about the education system. We unpacked our needfinding data into empathy maps and gained valuable insights.

Experience Prototyping

Using our empathy maps and needfinding data, we formulated points of views from our target user perspective and brainstormed how we might address their needs. We then created and tested 3 very rough experience prototypes to see if there were any assumptions about our users that were unaccounted for, as these could make our proposed solutions ineffective.

Concept Video

We brainstormed ideas about how a user could interact with our product and came up with some preliminary design ideas. Over the course of a week, we shot and edited a concept video which tentatively illustrates why our product is needed and how a user would use it on a high-level.

Lo-Fi Prototype

For the low-fi prototype, we made rough sketches of UIs for different devices such as tablets, phones, and computers. We then narrowed down our focus to tablets, which we felt would be more suitable for our audience do to projects with. We sketches and storyboarded the UI and tested it with both children and teachers.

Medium-Fi Prototype

Using our low-fi prototype sketches and storyboards, we built a medium-fi prototype using Marvel and Sketch, which lets you try out our product with hard-coded data. We made sure to incorporate feedback from our low-fi prototype usability tests into our medium-fi prototype.
